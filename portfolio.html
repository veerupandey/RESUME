Planner–Generator–Evaluator Diligence Harness Active Attraction
Realm (Sector): Private investment / M&A Operational Link: Sits directly on top of the PE Data Room Intelligence System
The Illusion (The Challenge)

Unlike software engineering—where a compiler serves as a cheap mechanical oracle for incorrect syntax—due diligence has no mechanical compiler. Large Language Models can easily produce highly fluent, professionally formatted investment committee (IC) memos that are subtly wrong on margin trajectories, customer concentration math, or working capital normalizations, leading to silent, high-impact analytical failures.

The Engineering (The Attraction)

Led a team of AI/ML and software engineers to design, build, and deploy a robust three-role **Planner–Generator–Evaluator** pattern. The Evaluator implements an opposing-oracle pattern (adapted from John Regehr's Zero Degrees of Freedom framework for differential software testing). Every single claim made in a generated diligence memo must reconcile against a verified, independent precision oracle: the Excel deal model, an interview transcript citation, or a raw source document from the deal room.

Role Responsibility
Planner Establishes analytical structure for a specific target — sections, cross-tabs, and relevant cohort comparison sets.
Generator Drafts memo content dynamically from indexed data rooms and historical model spreadsheets.
Evaluator Runs every draft claim through strict completeness, precision, and logical coherence checklists.
Evaluator Checks
  • Completeness: Memo covers every required section and appendix of the canonical IC template.
  • Precision: Every financial figure ties deterministically to a verified source document or Excel deal cell.
  • Coherence: Every management claim or operational narrative cites an actual interview transcript or signed document.

Reconciliation failures are routed back to the generator with explicit, machine-readable deltas to correct and redraft.

AI Video Pipeline Technical Diligence Active Attraction
Realm (Sector): Media / AI-Native Acquisition Target Imagineering Phase: Completed Diligence Assessment Report
The Illusion (The Challenge)

Conduct technical due diligence of an AI-driven feature film production slated for the Indian theatrical market—a mythological epic with IMAX-tier delivery ambitions. The target's pitch deck made four highly ambitious technical claims; diligence was required to evaluate each claim against actual engineering realities and the current market state.

Claims Assessed & Findings
Claim Diligence Finding
32-bit EXR pipeline Real infrastructure, but meaningful only if the base generator outputs High Dynamic Range (HDR). Since ComfyUI defaults to 8-bit, the Radiance plugin is required to maintain IEEE 754 fp32 data throughout the chain. Non-HDR models wrapping 8-bit plates inside a 32-bit container add no dynamic range. At present, only Luma Ray3 and LTX LumidVid output scene-linear fp16/HDR EXR; Sora 2, Kling 3.0, and Veo 3.1 do not output native HDR.
IMAX-compatible outputs Ambiguous. Aspect ratio cropping (1.43:1 or 1.90:1) is trivial, but matching IMAX pixel counts and XYZ color spaces at theatrical master specs (DCP 12-bit JPEG 2000 XYZ) is the bottleneck. Full 32-bit is unnecessary for theatrical delivery; the pipeline simply benefits from fp32 working space with a clean dither down at final DCP encode.
Multi-language lip-sync Highly commoditized capability easily executed via public Sync Labs, HeyGen, and ElevenLabs APIs. No proprietary technology moat.
ComfyUI motion reference Relies on standard open-source tools (AnimateDiff, ControlNet video, motion LoRAs). Represents competent execution, not a proprietary workflow moat.
Unaddressed Risks Identified character consistency at feature-length as the key unaddressed technical failure mode that stalled prior Indian AI cinematic series (e.g. JioHotstar).
Diligence Questions Generated
  • Which base models generate the raw plates and what are the per-shot economics?
  • Does the 32-bit handling rely on fxtdstudios Radiance or a custom OCIO configuration?
  • What is the upconvert and color-grading process for plates coming from non-HDR native generators (e.g., dark scenes, firelight)?
  • A/B test the Mahabharata-equivalent assets on a calibrated HDR reference monitor through Ray3 vs. Seedance vs. Wan.
